  • Shut down electrical power - lockout and tag out unit
  • Recover any existing refrigerant (if applicable) in accordance with EPA regulations
  • Break vacuum with dry nitrogen
  • Remove electrical wiring from defective compressor
  • Un-sweat refrigerant lines from defective compressor and liquid line filter/drier
  • Provide and install new filter drier (if existing, replace filter drier or drier core)
  • Install new OEM compressor and liquid line filter/drier and braze in refrigerant piping
  • Pressurize refrigerant system with dry nitrogen and trace gas - leak check newly installed components and brazing  joints
  • Evacuate unit vacuum to 250 microns Hg
  • Hold test vacuum to ensure integrity of refrigeration circuit
  • Reconnect electrical wiring to new OEM compressor (if additional wiring is needed, provide and install)
  • Provide and install new virgin refrigerant into compressor circuit
  • Start unit
  • Check compressor amperage and ensure proper operation
  • Balance refrigerant charge to get correct liquid line sub-cooling
  • Ensure proper operation of new compressor and unit
  • Return to job site within 30 days and confirm proper operation and perform quick acid test to verify if filter drier needs to be replaced a second time
